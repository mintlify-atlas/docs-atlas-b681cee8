## Overview

Both Lapse.iso and RemoteJarLoader.iso must be burned to a Blu-ray disc before they can be used on your PlayStation 4. The burning process varies by operating system.

<Warning>
  Use **Blu-ray discs** (BD-R or BD-RE), not DVDs. The PS4 requires Blu-ray media for BD-J applications.
</Warning>

## Prerequisites

- A Blu-ray disc burner (internal or external)
- Blank BD-R or BD-RE discs
- The ISO file you want to burn (Lapse.iso or RemoteJarLoader.iso)

<Note>
  BD-RE (rewritable) discs are recommended for testing, as they can be erased and reused. BD-R discs are write-once.
</Note>

## Platform-Specific Instructions

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Windows">
    ### Windows: Using ImgBurn

    **ImgBurn** is the recommended tool for burning ISO files on Windows.

    <Steps>
      <Step title="Download ImgBurn">
        Download ImgBurn from the official website: [https://www.imgburn.com](https://www.imgburn.com)
        
        Install the application following the setup wizard.
      </Step>

      <Step title="Launch ImgBurn">
        Open ImgBurn and select **"Write image file to disc"** (or press Ctrl+W).
      </Step>

      <Step title="Select Your ISO">
        Click the **Browse** button next to "Source" and select your ISO file:
        - `Lapse.iso`
        - `RemoteJarLoader.iso`
      </Step>

      <Step title="Insert Blank Disc">
        Insert a blank BD-R or BD-RE disc into your Blu-ray burner.
        
        Wait for ImgBurn to recognize the disc.
      </Step>

      <Step title="Configure Burn Settings">
        Recommended settings:
        - **Write Speed**: Select a slower speed (4x or 6x) for better reliability
        - **Verify**: Check "Verify" to ensure the disc burned correctly
      </Step>

      <Step title="Burn the Disc">
        Click the **Write** button (large disc icon) to begin burning.
        
        Wait for the burn process to complete. ImgBurn will verify the disc if you enabled that option.
      </Step>

      <Step title="Finalize">
        Once complete, eject the disc. Your Blu-ray disc is ready to use on your PS4.
      </Step>
    </Steps>

    <Note>
      ImgBurn may prompt you to install additional software during setup. Be careful to decline any bundled offers that are not related to ImgBurn itself.
    </Note>
  </Tab>

  <Tab title="Linux">
    ### Linux: Using K3b

    **K3b** is a comprehensive disc burning application for Linux with full Blu-ray support.

    <Steps>
      <Step title="Install K3b">
        Install K3b using your distribution's package manager:
        
        <CodeGroup>
        ```bash Ubuntu/Debian
        sudo apt install k3b
        ```
        
        ```bash Fedora
        sudo dnf install k3b
        ```
        
        ```bash Arch Linux
        sudo pacman -S k3b
        ```
        </CodeGroup>
      </Step>

      <Step title="Launch K3b">
        Open K3b from your application menu or run:
        
        ```bash
        k3b
        ```
      </Step>

      <Step title="Start Burn Image">
        From the menu, select:
        - **Tools** → **Burn Image...**
        
        Or click the **Burn Image** button on the toolbar.
      </Step>

      <Step title="Select Your ISO">
        Click **Browse** and navigate to your ISO file:
        - `Lapse.iso`
        - `RemoteJarLoader.iso`
        
        K3b will automatically detect the ISO format.
      </Step>

      <Step title="Insert Blank Disc">
        Insert a blank BD-R or BD-RE disc into your Blu-ray burner.
        
        K3b will detect the disc type and capacity.
      </Step>

      <Step title="Configure Burn Settings">
        Recommended settings:
        - **Writing Speed**: Select 4x or 6x for reliability
        - **Verify written data**: Enable this option
        - **Simulation**: Disable (unless testing)
      </Step>

      <Step title="Burn the Disc">
        Click **Start** to begin burning the ISO.
        
        K3b will show progress and verify the disc after burning.
      </Step>

      <Step title="Complete">
        When finished, eject the disc. It's ready for your PS4.
      </Step>
    </Steps>

    <Note>
      K3b is part of the KDE project but works on any Linux desktop environment. You may need to install additional KDE dependencies.
    </Note>

    ### Alternative: Command Line

    For advanced users, you can burn ISOs using `cdrecord` or `growisofs`:

    ```bash
    # Find your burner device
    wodim --devices

    # Burn the ISO (replace /dev/sr0 with your device)
    growisofs -dvd-compat -Z /dev/sr0=Lapse.iso
    ```
  </Tab>

  <Tab title="macOS">
    ### macOS: Using BD-SimpleBurn

    **BD-SimpleBurn** is a specialized tool for burning Blu-ray discs on macOS.

    <Steps>
      <Step title="Download BD-SimpleBurn">
        Download BD-SimpleBurn from GitHub:
        
        [https://github.com/C4ndyF1sh/BD-SimpleBurn](https://github.com/C4ndyF1sh/BD-SimpleBurn)
        
        Download the latest release `.dmg` file.
      </Step>

      <Step title="Install the Application">
        Open the `.dmg` file and drag BD-SimpleBurn to your Applications folder.
        
        <Warning>
          You may need to allow the app in **System Settings** → **Privacy & Security** if macOS blocks it.
        </Warning>
      </Step>

      <Step title="Launch BD-SimpleBurn">
        Open BD-SimpleBurn from your Applications folder.
        
        Grant any necessary permissions when prompted.
      </Step>

      <Step title="Insert Blank Disc">
        Insert a blank BD-R or BD-RE disc into your Blu-ray burner.
        
        Wait for macOS to recognize the disc.
      </Step>

      <Step title="Select Your ISO">
        In BD-SimpleBurn:
        - Click **Browse** or drag and drop your ISO file
        - Select `Lapse.iso` or `RemoteJarLoader.iso`
      </Step>

      <Step title="Configure Settings">
        Recommended settings:
        - **Burn Speed**: Select 4x or 6x for reliability
        - **Verify**: Enable disc verification
      </Step>

      <Step title="Burn the Disc">
        Click **Burn** to start the burning process.
        
        BD-SimpleBurn will show progress and verify the disc after completion.
      </Step>

      <Step title="Finish">
        Eject the disc when complete. Your Blu-ray is ready for the PS4.
      </Step>
    </Steps>

    <Note>
      External Blu-ray burners are more common on macOS, as many Macs don't have built-in optical drives. Ensure your burner is compatible with macOS.
    </Note>
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## Burn Settings Recommendations

For optimal compatibility with PS4:

- **Burn Speed**: 4x-6x (slower is more reliable)
- **Verification**: Always enable to catch burn errors
- **Disc Type**: BD-R for permanent, BD-RE for testing
- **Format**: ISO9660/UDF (automatic for ISO files)

## Troubleshooting

### Burn Fails or Errors

- Try a slower burn speed (2x or 4x)
- Use a different brand of blank discs
- Clean your burner's lens
- Update your burner's firmware

### PS4 Doesn't Recognize Disc

- Verify the ISO burned correctly (use verification option)
- Try burning at a slower speed
- Ensure you're using BD-R/BD-RE, not DVD media
- Clean the disc and PS4's disc reader

### Disc Verification Fails

- The burn may be corrupted; try again with a new disc
- Reduce burn speed to 2x or 4x
- Check that your ISO file isn't corrupted (verify checksum)

## Disc Care

- Handle discs by the edges only
- Store in protective cases when not in use
- Keep discs away from heat, moisture, and direct sunlight
- Use a soft, lint-free cloth to clean if needed (wipe from center outward)
