## What is Lapse.iso?

Lapse.iso is a Blu-ray disc image with a built-in Lapse JAR payload. When burned to a disc and inserted into your PS4, it automatically loads your homebrew enabler payload from USB and sets up the exploit environment.

### How It Works

Lapse.iso performs the following actions:

- Automatically loads `payload.bin` from your USB drive
- Copies the payload to `/data/payload.bin` on the PS4 internal storage
- Starts BinLoader listening on port **9020** (even if payload.bin is not found)

<Note>
  Lapse.iso is designed for firmware versions **9.00 – 12.02**. It will not work on PS5.
</Note>

## Complete Workflow

<Steps>
  <Step title="Prepare Your USB Drive">
    Format your USB drive as **exFAT** or **FAT32**.
    
    Place your homebrew enabler payload (e.g., GoldHEN or ps4-hen) at the root of the USB drive and rename it to `payload.bin`.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Configure PS4 Settings">
    Before running the exploit, configure these settings on your PS4:
    
    - **Enable HDCP** in Settings (required for Blu-ray playback)
    - Turn off **"Disable Pop-ups while playing video"** in notification settings to receive notifications
    
    <Warning>
      If Blu-ray playback is not yet activated (first time using a Blu-ray disc):
      - **Disable Automatic Updates** first to avoid firmware upgrades
      - Connect to the internet once to activate Blu-ray playback
    </Warning>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Burn the ISO to Disc">
    Burn Lapse.iso to a Blu-ray disc using the appropriate tool for your platform. See [Burning Blu-ray Discs](/guides/burning-iso) for detailed instructions.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Insert USB First">
    Insert your prepared USB drive into the PS4.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Insert the Blu-ray Disc">
    Insert the burned Lapse.iso disc into your PS4.
    
    The PS4 will begin reading the disc and loading the BD-J application.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Wait for Payload Delivery">
    The exploit will automatically:
    
    - Load `payload.bin` from your USB drive
    - Copy it to `/data/payload.bin`
    - Execute your homebrew enabler
    
    You should see notifications confirming successful payload loading.
  </Step>

  <Step title="If Exploit Fails">
    If the exploit doesn't work:
    
    - **Restart the PS4** completely before retrying
    - Do **not** simply reopen the BD-J app — stability will drop significantly
  </Step>
</Steps>

## What Happens During Exploit

When you insert the Lapse.iso disc, the PS4's Blu-ray Java (BD-J) environment loads the embedded Lapse JAR payload. This payload:

1. Searches for `payload.bin` on your USB drive
2. Reads the payload into memory
3. Copies it to the PS4's `/data/` directory for persistence
4. Triggers the exploit chain to gain code execution
5. Launches your homebrew enabler (GoldHEN, ps4-hen, etc.)
6. Starts BinLoader on port 9020 for additional payloads

<Note>
  The BD-J environment provides a bridge between the restricted Blu-ray application context and native PS4 code execution through carefully crafted exploit primitives.
</Note>

## Additional Features

### BinLoader Port

After the initial payload loads, BinLoader remains active on port **9020**. You can send additional `.bin` payloads to this port using network tools.

### USB Fallback

If the USB drive is not plugged in during exploit execution, Lapse will attempt to load from `/data/payload.bin` if it exists from a previous run.

## Troubleshooting

- **No notification appears**: Ensure "Disable Pop-ups while playing video" is turned OFF
- **Exploit doesn't trigger**: Restart the PS4 completely and try again
- **USB not detected**: Verify the drive is formatted as exFAT or FAT32
- **Payload doesn't load**: Check that your payload is named exactly `payload.bin`
